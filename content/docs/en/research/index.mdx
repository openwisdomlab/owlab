## The Intellectual DNA of OWLab

If the [Knowledge Base](/docs/core) is OWLab's "skeleton" -- providing execution-level methodologies and operational manuals -- then the **Frontier Philosophy modules (L Series)** are OWLab's "DNA" -- defining the organization's core beliefs, values, and intellectual boundaries.

<Callout type="info">
**Positioning**: The L Series is the "Way" (Dao), while the M Series is the "Method" (Shu). Philosophy guides practice; practice validates philosophy -- together they form a complete cognitive loop.
</Callout>

### Why a "Philosophy Layer"?

In practice, we have found that many maker spaces invest enormous effort in "how to do it" while neglecting the fundamental question of "why do it this way." The result is:

- **Form without substance**: Spaces have equipment but lack soul
- **Following trends**: Chasing fads rather than making principled decisions
- **Difficulty sustaining**: Without a clear vision, resolve falters when challenges arise

The L Series exists to answer the most fundamental questions: **How should spaces shape people? How should technology serve learning? How does wisdom emerge within communities?**

### Mapping Between L Series and M Series

Each L module has corresponding M modules as its "implementation vehicle." The philosophy layer provides the "why" and "what is good," while the execution layer provides the "how" and "concrete steps."

| L Series (Philosophy) | M Series (Execution) | Relationship |
|----------------------|---------------------|-------------|
| **L01 Space as Educator** | M03 Space, M06 Safety | Neuroarchitecture principles --> Physical space design standards |
| **L02 Extended Mind** | M04 Programs, M05 Tools | Cognitive exoskeleton concept --> AI-assisted learning practice |
| **L03 Emergent Wisdom** | M02 Governance, M08 Operations | Collective intelligence mechanisms --> Community operations strategy |
| **L04 Poetics of Technology** | M05 Tools, M00 Meta | Philosophy of technology --> Open-source hardware selection |

### Relationship with the Knowledge Base

The two documentation systems serve different cognitive stages. The Knowledge Base provides practice-validated reliable methods suited for practitioners seeking solutions. The Living Modules track theories still in evolution, providing thinking frameworks for innovators willing to explore the unknown. They are not substitutes but complements -- philosophy needs practice for validation, and practice needs philosophy for breakthroughs.

| Dimension | Knowledge Base (M Series) | Frontier Philosophy (L Series) |
|-----------|--------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Content Nature | Validated practical knowledge | Cutting-edge research ideas |
| Update Frequency | Stable, occasional updates | Active, continuously evolving |
| Certainty | High, directly applicable | Low-medium, requires experimental validation |
| Reader Mindset | "Tell me how to do it" | "Inspire me how to think" |
| Cross-references | Cites L modules for theoretical support | Cites M modules for practical grounding |

## Four Philosophy Modules

### 01 Space as Educator
**Neuroarchitecture and Innovation Environment Design**

> [Enter Module -->](./01-space-as-educator)

**Meta-metaphor**: Space shapes people, people shape space -- a bidirectional process where space participates in creation rather than merely containing it.

**Frontier Directions**: These research directions span neuroscience, architecture, environmental psychology, and other fields, collectively exploring how the physical environment influences human cognition, behavior, and creativity. From micro-level neural activity to macro-level social interaction, we are building the scientific foundation behind space design.

- Neuroarchitecture
- Third Space 2.0
- Affordance Ecology
- Timescape
- Theater of Failure
- Boundary Dissolution

---

### 02 Extended Mind
**AI-Enhanced Cognition and Learning Science**

> [Enter Module -->](./02-extended-mind)

**Meta-metaphor**: AI is a cognitive exoskeleton -- extending human capacity rather than replacing human thought.

**Frontier Directions**: As generative AI becomes an everyday tool, the core question shifts from "can we use AI" to "how do we maintain and develop uniquely human cognitive abilities while AI-assisted." These studies explore the optimal balance of human-machine collaboration, attempting to answer when to use, how to use, and when not to use AI.

- Cognitive Offloading Theory
- Socratic AI
- Collaborative Intelligence Emergence
- Metacognitive Dimensions of AI Literacy
- Educational Value of Generative Hallucinations

---

### 03 Emergent Wisdom
**Community Dynamics and Collaborative Cognition**

> [Enter Module -->](./03-emergent-wisdom)

**Meta-metaphor**: Communities are incubators of wisdom -- individual wisdom is amplified, recombined, and evolved within communities.

**Frontier Directions**: Collective intelligence is not a simple summation of individuals but an emergent phenomenon under specific community structures and dynamic conditions. Researchers are uncovering the mechanisms that promote or inhibit this emergence, from the neural basis of psychological safety to optimal configurations of cognitive diversity, from the information value of weak ties to the boundary-spanning functions of boundary objects.

- Weak Ties 2.0
- Cognitive Diversity Engineering
- Neural Basis of Psychological Safety
- Knowledge Boundary Objects
- Antifragile Communities

Each module follows a unified four-layer structure:

```text
1. Core Philosophy
   |-- Meta-metaphor: A central concept unifying the entire module
   +-- Paradigm Shift: The leap from old thinking to new thinking

2. Frontier Research
   |-- Key papers/studies (continuously updated)
   |-- Interdisciplinary connections
   +-- Hypotheses awaiting verification

3. Design Principles
   |-- Actionable principles derived from philosophy
   +-- Anti-pattern warnings

4. Implications
   |-- Guidance for lab construction
   +-- Connections with other modules
```

## How to Use the Living Modules

### As Inspiration

When designing innovation spaces and uncertain about how to make decisions, come here for a **starting point for thinking**. The living modules will not give you answers, but they will give you angles for asking questions.

### As Theoretical Support

When you need **theoretical justification** for design decisions, cite the frontier research in the living modules. But be sure to note the "frontier/awaiting verification" status of the research.

### As an Update Subscription

The living modules continuously track cutting-edge developments. Visit periodically to stay current on **the latest research trends** in each field.

## Update Mechanism

The living modules use an AI-assisted + human-reviewed update model:

1. **AI Tracking**: Periodic scanning of new research in relevant fields
2. **Content Drafting**: AI generates update drafts
3. **Human Review**: Expert review ensures quality
4. **Version Release**: Formally published after review

Each module's `research-updates.mdx` file records the latest developments.

## Scientific Integrity Statement

<Callout type="warn">
**On Evidence Levels**: L Series content involves frontier research and theoretical exploration, with maturity and certainty levels different from the practical knowledge of the M Series. Please note the following distinctions:
</Callout>

### Content Classification

| Type | Label | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **Scientific Consensus** | No special label | Conclusions based on peer-reviewed literature verified by multiple replicated studies |
| **Frontier Research** | `[Frontier]` | Recently published research requiring further validation |
| **Theoretical Hypothesis** | `[Hypothesis]` | Logically derived possibilities without empirical support |
| **Philosophical Stance** | `[Philosophy]` | Claims at the level of values and worldview, not empirical questions |

### Citation Standards

Scholars and works cited in the L Series are all genuine academic resources:

**Core Theoretical Foundations**:
- **Clark, A. & Chalmers, D. (1998)**. *The Extended Mind*. Analysis 58(1), 7-19. -- The foundational paper on extended mind theory
- **Pentland, A. (2014)**. *Social Physics*. Penguin Press. -- The founding work of social physics
- **Weiser, M. (1991)**. *The Computer for the 21st Century*. Scientific American. -- Origin of the "calm technology" concept
- **Edmondson, A. (1999)**. *Psychological Safety and Learning Behavior in Work Teams*. Administrative Science Quarterly. -- The classic study on psychological safety
- **Gibson, J.J. (1979)**. *The Ecological Approach to Visual Perception*. -- Foundation of affordance theory

We commit to: **All citations are based on genuine peer-reviewed literature or classic philosophical works**, with zero fabricated references. If you find any errors, please report them via GitHub Issues.
