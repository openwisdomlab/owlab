## Building Standards

<Callout type="info">
The core of M05 is "tools actually being used." Assessment criteria are designed around **equipment utilization, trinity coverage, and maintenance participation**, ensuring tools truly become extensions of creation rather than display pieces.
</Callout>

### Core Principles

| ID | Core Principle | One-Liner | Practice Signals |
|----|---------------|-----------|-----------------|
| **N1** | Value Through Use | Equipment utilization matters more than equipment grade | Core equipment weekly usage >= 50% |
| **N2** | Trinity | AI + open-source hardware + digital fabrication, all essential | All three tool types actively used |
| **N3** | Software Defines Hardware | Mastering the software tool chain matters more than owning hardware | Students can independently use design software |
| **N4** | Maintenance as Education | Equipment maintenance is a learning opportunity, not a burden | Students participate in equipment maintenance |
| **N5** | AI as Amplifier | AI amplifies creativity rather than replacing thinking | After AI assistance, students still need to think |

### Mandatory Standards (10 items)

| ID | Standard | Why It Matters | Quantitative Metric | Tier |
|----|----------|---------------|---------------------|------|
| **S1** | Core Equipment Operational | Basic infrastructure assurance | 3D printers/electronics kits/computers 100% operational | Foundation |
| **S2** | Safety Equipment Complete | Safety is the baseline | Fire extinguishers, first aid kits, safety goggles 100% equipped | Foundation |
| **S3** | Equipment Training Coverage | Must know how to use it | Core equipment training process covers 100% of users | Foundation |
| **S4** | Consumables Inventory Management | Equipment without consumables = unusable | Common consumables inventory warning system established | Foundation |
| **S5** | Equipment Ledger Complete | Management with verifiable records | Equipment list, status, and maintenance records 100% auditable | Foundation |
| **S6** | Trinity Coverage | Complete tool system | AI tools + open-source hardware + digital fabrication, each >= 1 in active use | Growth |
| **S7** | Equipment Utilization Rate | Value through use | Core equipment weekly average usage >= 50% | Growth |
| **S8** | Learner Maintenance Participation | Maintenance as education | >= 30% of learners have participated in equipment maintenance or upkeep | Growth |
| **S9** | Open-Source Priority | Open ecosystem, continuous growth | >= 70% of hardware platforms are open-source (Arduino/Raspberry Pi, etc.) | Excellence |
| **S10** | Equipment Upgrade Iteration | Keeping pace with the times | Annual equipment needs assessment with an upgrade plan | Excellence |

### Parameter Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Healthy Range | Warning Signal |
|--------|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Equipment Utilization Rate** | Actual use time / Available time x 100% | 50%-80% | &lt;30% equipment may become display pieces |
| **Equipment Availability Rate** | Normally functioning devices / Total devices x 100% | >= 90% | &lt;80% maintenance may be lagging |
| **Maintenance Participation Rate** | Learners who participated in maintenance / Total learners x 100% | >= 30% | &lt;15% "maintenance as education" not implemented |

### Extension Patterns

| Pattern | Applicable Context | Core Practice | Expected Outcome |
|---------|-------------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Equipment Certification Officers** | Deepening maintenance education | Train learners as "certification officers" for specific equipment, responsible for training newcomers and routine maintenance | Learners develop ownership; maintenance quality improves |
| **Tool Chain Workshops** | Strengthening software capabilities | Regular workshops on AI tools/design software, reinforcing "software defines hardware" capability | Learners can independently complete the full design-to-fabrication workflow |

**AI Usage Reflection Questions**

When using AI tools for learning, ask yourself:
1. After handing this task to AI, do I still need to think?
2. Can I understand the AI's output? Can I judge if it's correct?
3. Without AI, could I complete this a slower way?
4. What capability is AI amplifying for me? What thinking is it replacing?

This module provides equipment selection and management standards for OWL laboratories. We don't pursue the most expensive industrial-grade equipment, but rather **high availability**, **low barriers (Low Floor)**, and **vast possibilities (High Ceiling)**. The core principle: equipment selection should serve educational goals, not technological showmanship.
