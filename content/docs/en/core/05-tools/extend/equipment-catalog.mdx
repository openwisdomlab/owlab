# Fab Lab Core Equipment Standards

## Overview

A Fab Lab (Fabrication Laboratory) is more than a collection of equipment — it is a node in a global innovation network. The equipment configuration follows a philosophy: enabling anyone to make (almost) anything, while connecting to a worldwide knowledge community.

**Guiding Principles**:
- **Low floor, high ceiling**: Equipment should be accessible to beginners yet capable enough for advanced projects
- **Global interoperability**: Core capabilities ensure projects can be replicated at any Fab Lab worldwide
- **Educational alignment**: Equipment selection serves learning objectives, not just production goals

---

## Part One: Fab Lab Design Philosophy

### 1.1 Fab Charter Core Principles

The Fab Charter establishes the fundamental values of the network [E1]:

| Principle | Description | Implication for OWL |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Access** | Labs provide access for invention | Open access policies |
| **Learning** | Training in the lab is available | Structured learning pathways |
| **Responsibility** | Users must ensure safety and cleanliness | User responsibility covenant |
| **Secrecy** | Designs and processes must be available for others to learn from | Open knowledge sharing |
| **Business** | Commercial activities must not conflict with open access | Clear commercial guidelines |
| **Network** | Fab Labs are a global knowledge-sharing network | Global connectivity |

### 1.2 "Low Floor, High Ceiling" Equipment Selection Principle

| Parameter | Low Floor (Entry) | High Ceiling (Advanced) |
|-----------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Cost** | Affordable for education budgets | Professional-grade capability |
| **Learning curve** | Usable within first session | Years to master |
| **Safety** | Safe for supervised youth | Industrial-grade safety systems |
| **Output quality** | Functional prototypes | Near-production quality |
| **Software** | Free/open source available | Professional software compatible |

## Part Three: Core Equipment Detailed Specifications

### 3.1 Laser Cutter

#### Technical Specifications Comparison

| Parameter | Education Entry | Education Standard | Professional |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Work area** | 300x200mm | 600x400mm | 600x900mm+ |
| **Power** | 40W CO2 | 60-80W CO2 | 100W+ CO2 |
| **Materials** | Wood, acrylic, paper | + leather, fabric | + thick materials |
| **Software** | LightBurn, LaserGRBL | LightBurn, RDWorks | Industrial CAM |

#### Education Scenario Recommendations

| Age Group | Recommended Use | Safety Level |
|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| K-J1 (4-9) | Observation only; pre-cut materials | Demonstration only |
| J2 (10-12) | Supervised operation after training | Adult supervision required |
| T1-T2 (13-18) | Independent use after certification | Certified independent use |

#### Supporting Facilities

- Exhaust ventilation system (mandatory)
- Fire extinguisher within reach
- Safety interlock on lid
- First aid kit nearby
- Clear operating procedures posted

### 3.2 3D Printer

#### Technology Comparison

| Technology | FDM | SLA/DLP | SLS |
|-----------|-----|---------|-----|
| **Principle** | Filament extrusion | UV resin curing | Powder sintering |
| **Cost** | Low-Medium | Medium | High |
| **Resolution** | 100-200 microns | 25-50 microns | 100 microns |
| **Materials** | PLA, PETG, ABS | Resin | Nylon, TPU |
| **Safety** | Low risk (PLA) | Chemical handling | Powder handling |
| **Education fit** | Excellent | Good (13+) | Limited (18+) |

#### FDM 3D Printer Specifications

| Parameter | Entry Level | Standard | Advanced |
|-----------|------------|----------|----------|
| **Build volume** | 220x220x250mm | 256x256x256mm | 350x350x400mm |
| **Nozzle temp** | Up to 260°C | Up to 300°C | Up to 300°C |
| **Bed temp** | Up to 80°C | Up to 110°C | Up to 110°C |
| **Auto-leveling** | Basic | Advanced | Advanced |
| **Enclosure** | Open | Optional | Enclosed |

#### Education Scenario Recommendations
- Start with PLA filament (safe, biodegradable, no heated enclosure needed)
- Multi-color capability motivates younger learners
- Emphasize design iteration over print quality
- Build failure analysis into the learning process

### 3.3 CNC Machining Equipment

#### Equipment Types

| Type | Capability | Size | Suited For |
|------|-----------|------|-----------|
| **Desktop CNC** | Light materials (wood, foam, PCB) | Small footprint | Education, PCB milling |
| **CNC Router** | Wood, plastics, soft metals | Medium-large | Furniture, enclosures |
| **CNC Mill** | Metals, precision parts | Medium | Advanced manufacturing |

#### Safety Configuration Requirements

- Emergency stop button
- Spindle guard/enclosure
- Dust collection system
- Eye and ear protection
- Age restriction: 18+ for CNC routers/mills

### 3.4 Electronics Prototyping Equipment

#### Electronics Workstation Configuration

| Component | Entry Level | Standard |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| **Soldering** | Temperature-controlled iron | Hot air station + iron |
| **Measurement** | Basic multimeter | Multimeter + oscilloscope |
| **Power supply** | USB power | Bench power supply |
| **Magnification** | Handheld magnifier | Stereo microscope |
| **PCB** | Breadboards | PCB mill or etching |

#### Microcontroller Platform Selection

| Platform | Age Group | Language | Strengths |
|----------|----------|----------|-----------|
| **micro:bit** | J1-J2 (7-12) | Blocks/Python | Simple, built-in sensors |
| **Arduino** | J2-T2 (10-18) | C/C++ | Versatile, huge ecosystem |
| **Raspberry Pi** | T1-A (13+) | Python | Full computer, AI capable |
| **ESP32** | T1-A (13+) | C/Arduino/MicroPython | WiFi/BLE, IoT projects |

### 3.5 Traditional Manufacturing Tools

#### Basic Toolkit Configuration

| Category | Tools | Purpose |
|----------|-------|---------|
| **Hand tools** | Screwdrivers, pliers, wrenches, hammers | Assembly, adjustment |
| **Measuring** | Rulers, calipers, tape measures | Precision measurement |

## Part Four: Tiered Configuration Plans

### 4.1 Lite Tier (Explorer Level)

**Budget**: $5,000-15,000 | **Space**: 30-50 m² | **Suited for**: After-school programs, small makerspaces

| Equipment | Quantity | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| 3D printer (FDM entry) | 1-2 | PLA focus |
| Basic electronics kit | 5-10 sets | Arduino/micro:bit |
| Hand tools set | 1 set | Basic toolkit |
| Computer workstation | 2-3 | CAD capable |
| Craft supplies | Assorted | Paper, cardboard, tape |

### 4.2 Pro Tier (Standard Level)

**Budget**: $15,000-50,000 | **Space**: 50-100 m² | **Suited for**: School labs, community makerspaces

| Equipment | Quantity | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| 3D printer (FDM standard) | 2-4 | Multi-material |
| Laser cutter (40-60W) | 1 | With exhaust system |
| Electronics workstation | 5-10 | Soldering + breadboards |
| Vinyl cutter | 1 | Stickers, circuits |
| Computer workstations | 5-8 | CAD/CAM capable |
| Hand/power tools | Full set | Including drill press |

### 4.3 Ultra Tier (Challenge Level)

**Budget**: $50,000-150,000+ | **Space**: 100-200+ m² | **Suited for**: Full Fab Labs, innovation centers

| Equipment | Quantity | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| 3D printers (multi-tech) | 4-8 | FDM + SLA |
| Laser cutter (60-100W) | 1-2 | Large format |
| CNC router | 1 | With dust collection |
| PCB mill | 1 | Circuit board production |
| Electronics lab | Full | Oscilloscopes, power supplies |
| Woodworking tools | Full set | Including table saw (18+) |
| Computer workstations | 10+ | Professional software |

## Part Five: Equipment Procurement and Selection Guide

### 5.1 Procurement Decision Matrix

| Factor | Weight | Evaluation Method |
|--------|--------|------------------|
| **Educational value** | 30% | Alignment with curriculum goals |
| **Safety** | 25% | Risk assessment, age appropriateness |
| **Total cost of ownership** | 20% | Purchase + maintenance + consumables |
| **Reliability** | 15% | Brand reputation, warranty, support |
| **Scalability** | 10% | Upgrade path, compatibility |

### 5.2 Safety Certification Requirements

| Standard | Region | Applies to |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| **CE** | Europe | All electrical equipment |
| **UL** | North America | Electrical safety |
| **CCC** | China | Mandatory certification |
| **OSHA** | US | Workplace safety compliance |

## Part Six: Equipment Management Best Practices

### 6.1 Asset Lifecycle Management

```text
Procurement → Installation → Training → Operation → Maintenance → Upgrade/Retire
     │            │            │           │            │              │
  Vendor eval  Setup guide  User cert   Usage log   PM schedule   Disposal plan
```

### 6.2 User Training System

| Level | Content | Duration | Required For |
|-------|---------|----------|-------------|
| **C0: Safety basics** | General lab safety | 30 min | All users |
| **C1: Equipment category** | Specific equipment type | 1-2 hours | Equipment users |
| **C2: Advanced certification** | Independent operation | 2-4 hours | Unsupervised use |

### 6.3 Maintenance Schedule

| Frequency | Tasks |
|-----------|-------|
| **Daily** | Visual inspection, cleaning, consumables check |
| **Weekly** | Calibration check, alignment, lubrication |
| **Monthly** | Deep clean, belt/bearing inspection, software updates |
| **Quarterly** | Professional service, safety system test |

## Common Misconceptions

1. **"Buy the most expensive equipment"** — Educational value matters more than specs. A well-used entry-level printer teaches more than an idle professional machine.
2. **"More equipment = better lab"** — A few well-maintained, well-supported machines beat many neglected ones.
3. **"Equipment first, curriculum second"** — Start with learning goals; equipment should serve the curriculum, not the other way around.
