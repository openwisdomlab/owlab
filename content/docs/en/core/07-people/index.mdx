## Building Standards

<Callout type="info">
The core of M07 is "ignite, not pour in." Assessment criteria are designed around **whether mentors are learning, whether guidance empowers, and whether the team is growing**, making "people" truly the laboratory's most important asset.
</Callout>

### Core Principles

| ID | Core Principle | One-Liner | Practice Signals |
|----|---------------|-----------|-----------------|
| **N1** | Mentors Are Learners First | Demonstrate to students that you are learning too | Mentors say "I don't know either — let's look it up together" |
| **N2** | Scaffolded Guidance | Provide just-enough support | Learners feel "I did it myself" |
| **N3** | Clear Growth Pathway | Everyone knows the next step in their growth | E-level certification system operational |
| **N4** | Diverse and Inclusive | All backgrounds can teach | Team has diverse capabilities and perspectives |
| **N5** | Community-Driven | Volunteers are a core force | Active volunteer participation |

### Mandatory Standards (10 items)

| ID | Standard | Why It Matters | Quantitative Metric | Tier |
|----|----------|---------------|---------------------|------|
| **S1** | Mentor Qualification Coverage | People make it work | >= 1 E1-certified or above mentor | Foundation |
| **S2** | Onboarding Training Complete | Align philosophy and methods | 100% of new mentors complete onboarding training | Foundation |
| **S3** | Safety Training Coverage | Safety is the baseline | 100% of all mentors complete safety training | Foundation |
| **S4** | Mentor Learning Records | Mentors are learners first | >= 12 documented mentor learning activities per year | Foundation |
| **S5** | Team Configuration | Healthy staffing structure | Mentor-to-learner ratio &lt;= 1:15 | Foundation |
| **S6** | E-Level Certification Operational | Visible growth pathway | Mentors achieved E-level advancement in the past year | Growth |
| **S7** | Scaffolded Guidance Practice | Empower, not replace | >= 70% of learners report "I found the answer myself" | Growth |
| **S8** | Active Volunteers | Community power participating | >= 3 active volunteers per month on average | Growth |
| **S9** | Grand Mentor Connections | Access to the real frontier | >= 2 Grand Mentor interactions per year | Excellence |
| **S10** | Mentor Development Output | From beneficiary to contributor | Develop >= 1 new mentor to E1 per year | Excellence |

### Parameter Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Healthy Range | Warning Signal |
|--------|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Mentor-to-Learner Ratio** | Number of mentors / Active learners | 1:8 - 1:15 | >1:20 guidance quality may decline |
| **Mentor Retention Rate** | Retained mentors / Beginning-of-year mentors x 100% | >= 70% | &lt;50% team stability issues |
| **Mentor Growth Rate** | Mentors achieving E-level advancement / Total mentors x 100% | 10%-30% | 0% growth pathway may be blocked |

### Extension Patterns

| Pattern | Applicable Context | Core Practice | Expected Outcome |
|---------|-------------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Mentor Learning Circle** | Strengthening mentor growth | Monthly sessions where mentors share learning insights, observe each other, and exchange guidance techniques | Mentors continue growing; learning community forms |
| **Scaffolding Workshop** | Improving guidance quality | Specialized training on scaffolded guidance methods through role-play and case analysis | Mentors master the art of "asking instead of telling" |

### Assessment Tools

**Quarterly Mentor Behavior Observation Checklist**

Observe mentor behavior during guidance sessions:

| Observation Item | Often | Sometimes | Rarely |
|-----------------|-------|-----------|--------|
| Guides through questions rather than giving direct answers | [] | [] | [] |
| Says "I don't know either, let's explore together" | [] | [] | [] |
| Lets learners try things hands-on | [] | [] | [] |
| Gives process encouragement rather than only judging results | [] | [] | [] |
| Shares something they are currently learning | [] | [] | [] |

**Scaffolded Guidance Self-Check**

```text
+-------------------------------------------------------------+
|  Scaffolded Guidance Check                                   |
+-------------------------------------------------------------+
|  Before Guidance:                                            |
|  [] Do I understand the learner's current ability level?     |
|  [] Have I identified the "zone of proximal development"     |
|     the learner needs to cross?                              |
+-------------------------------------------------------------+
|  During Guidance:                                            |
|  [] Am I guiding with questions rather than telling answers? |
|  [] Is my support "just enough" (not too much, not too       |
|     little)?                                                 |
|  [] Am I "removing the scaffold" when the learner can do     |
|     it independently?                                        |
+-------------------------------------------------------------+
|  After Guidance:                                             |
|  [] Does the learner feel "I did it myself"?                 |
|  [] Could the learner solve a similar problem independently  |
|     next time?                                               |
+-------------------------------------------------------------+
```

**Learner Feedback Survey (About Mentors)**

1. Does the mentor often guide your thinking with questions? (Rarely / Sometimes / Often)
2. When you encounter difficulties, does the mentor give answers directly or help you find methods? (Gives answers / Helps me find methods)
3. After completing a project, whose credit do you think it is? (Mainly the mentor / We did it together / Mainly me)
4. Has the mentor shared something they are currently learning? (No / Yes)

```text
Ideal results:
- Question guidance: >= 70% select "Often"
- Encountering difficulties: >= 70% select "Helps me find methods"
- Credit attribution: >= 60% select "Mainly me"
- Mentor learning: >= 50% select "Yes"
```

> **One-line summary**: Not teaching, but igniting — mentors are not answer providers but co-explorers of questions.

| 3E Dimension | How This Module Embodies It |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Enlighten** | Cultivate facilitators who can spark others' curiosity — **mentors are learners first**, demonstrating to students that they too are learning |
| **Empower** | Scaffolded guidance methods + E0-E3 capability certification system make "how to guide" learnable, practicable, and assessable |
| **Engage** | Connect scientists, entrepreneurs, and university researchers as Grand Mentors so students can interact with people truly working at the frontier |

A laboratory's most important asset is not equipment, but people. **People are the soul of a laboratory** — an excellent mentor can create magical learning experiences with the simplest tools. We are not looking for "know-it-all experts" but "fellow travelers who never stop learning" — **mentors are not answer providers but co-explorers of questions**.
