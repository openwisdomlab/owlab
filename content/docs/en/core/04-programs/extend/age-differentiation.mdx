# Age-Differentiated Design

## Overview

Learners of different ages have distinct cognitive characteristics, interest patterns, and learning needs. Good age-appropriate design is not simply about adjusting difficulty — it means designing learning experiences that truly fit each developmental stage. This document draws on developmental psychology theories to provide age-differentiated design guidelines for maker education.

**Core Principle**: Respect developmental patterns; design challenges within the Zone of Proximal Development.

---

## 1. Developmental Psychology Foundations

### 1.1 Piaget's Cognitive Development Stages

| Stage | Age | Characteristics | Educational Implications |
|-------|-----|----------------|------------------------|
| **Sensorimotor** | 0-2 | Understanding the world through senses and actions | Sensory experiences, physical manipulation |
| **Preoperational** | 2-7 | Symbolic thinking develops, egocentric | Gamification, concrete representations |
| **Concrete Operational** | 7-11 | Logical thinking emerges, needs concrete support | Hands-on operations, visible feedback |
| **Formal Operational** | 11+ | Abstract thinking, hypothetical reasoning | Abstract concepts, complex projects |

### 1.2 Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development

```text
                    ┌───────────────────────────┐
                    │      Too Difficult Zone    │
                    │  (Cannot complete even     │
                    │   with assistance)         │
                    ├───────────────────────────┤
     ← Scaffolding →│  Zone of Proximal         │ ← Achievable with help
                    │  Development (ZPD)         │
                    │  (Can complete with help)  │
                    ├───────────────────────────┤
                    │   Independent Zone         │
                    │  (Can complete without     │
                    │   assistance)              │
                    └───────────────────────────┘
```

### 1.3 Erikson's Psychosocial Development

| Stage | Age | Core Task | Educational Implications |
|-------|-----|-----------|------------------------|
| Industry vs. Inferiority | 6-12 | Developing competence | Achievement experiences, affirming progress |
| Identity vs. Role Confusion | 12-18 | Exploring self | Autonomous choices, identity exploration |
| Intimacy vs. Isolation | 18-25 | Building relationships | Collaborative projects, community belonging |

## 2. Age-Differentiation Framework

### 2.1 OWL Age Tiers

| Age Group | Code | Stage Name | Core Characteristics |
|-----------|------|-----------|---------------------|
| 4-6 years | K | Enlightenment | Sensory exploration, gamification |
| 7-9 years | J1 | Sprouting | Concrete operations, instant feedback |
| 10-12 years | J2 | Growth | Project completeness, collaboration |
| 13-15 years | T1 | Exploration | Abstract thinking, autonomy |
| 16-18 years | T2 | Development | Deep projects, innovation |
| 18+ years | A | Adult | Professional depth, self-directed |

### 2.2 Differentiation Design Dimensions

| Dimension | K (4-6) | J1 (7-9) | J2 (10-12) | T1 (13-15) | T2 (16-18) |
|-----------|---------|----------|------------|------------|------------|
| **Duration** | 30-60 min | 1-2 hours | 2-4 hours | 4-8 hours | 8-16+ hours |
| **Complexity** | Minimal | Simple | Moderate | Medium-high | High |
| **Autonomy** | Low | Medium-low | Medium | Medium-high | High |
| **Abstraction** | Concrete | Mostly concrete | Concrete + abstract | Mostly abstract | Abstract |
| **Scaffolding** | Very high | High | Medium | Medium-low | Low |

## 3. Design Guidelines by Age Group

### 3.1 Enlightenment Stage (K: Ages 4-6)

#### Developmental Characteristics

| Dimension | Characteristics | Design Implications |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Cognition** | Preoperational, egocentric | Personalized experiences |
| **Motor** | Fine motor developing | Prioritize gross motor activities |
| **Attention** | Short span, easily distracted | Short activities, frequent changes |
| **Social** | Parallel play predominant | Non-mandatory collaboration |
| **Language** | Still developing | Less talking, more doing |

#### Design Principles

| Principle | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Sensory first** | Learn through sensory experience | Touch, observe, listen |
| **Gamification** | Everything is a game | Story contexts, role play |
| **Instant feedback** | Actions produce immediate results | Press button, light turns on |
| **Easy success** | Easy to achieve a sense of accomplishment | Simple tasks, timely praise |
| **Parent involvement** | Parent-child co-creation | Parents participate together |

#### Activity Examples

| Activity | Duration | Content | Materials |
|----------|----------|---------|-----------|
| Paper circuit cards | 30 min | Apply conductive tape to light LEDs | Paper, conductive tape, LEDs, batteries |
| Playdough circuits | 40 min | Build circuits with conductive playdough | Conductive playdough, LEDs |
| Block building machines | 45 min | Build with large-grain blocks | LEGO Duplo or similar |

---

### 3.2 Sprouting Stage (J1: Ages 7-9)

#### Developmental Characteristics

| Dimension | Characteristics | Design Implications |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Cognition** | Concrete operational beginning | Concrete operational support |
| **Logic** | Sequential logic emerging | Simple cause-and-effect |
| **Motor** | Fine motor developing | Can do precise operations |
| **Social** | Group awareness developing | Simple collaboration |
| **Self-esteem** | Critical period for competence | Achievement experiences |

#### Design Principles

| Principle | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Concrete & visible** | Thinking needs concrete support | Visualization, physical manipulation |
| **Timely feedback** | See results of actions | Code runs, immediate output |
| **Clear steps** | Explicit operational steps | Illustrated instructions, checklists |
| **Multiple choices** | Limited options | Choose colors, characters, etc. |
| **Positive reinforcement** | Affirm effort and progress | Badges, work showcases |

#### Activity Examples

| Activity | Duration | Content | Tools |
|----------|----------|---------|-------|
| Scratch animation | 90 min | Create simple animation | Scratch Jr or Scratch |
| LED circuits | 60 min | Series/parallel circuit exploration | Breadboard, LEDs, resistors |
| Simple machines | 90 min | Gear transmission, levers | LEGO Technic |

---

### 3.3 Growth Stage (J2: Ages 10-12)

#### Developmental Characteristics

| Dimension | Characteristics | Design Implications |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Cognition** | Mature concrete operations | Can handle more complexity |
| **Planning** | Initial planning ability | Introduction to project management |
| **Collaboration** | Strong team awareness | Group projects |
| **Achievement** | Industry vs. Inferiority critical period | Emphasize sense of achievement |
| **Interests** | Interest differentiation | Offer diverse options |

#### Design Principles

| Principle | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Complete projects** | Experience full project cycles | Design → Make → Present |
| **Collaborative learning** | Teamwork | Groups of 2-4 |
| **Bounded autonomy** | Choices within frameworks | Select project themes |
| **Multiple pathways** | Support different completion methods | Same function, different form |
| **Showcase & share** | Output display | Exhibitions, presentations |

#### Activity Examples

| Activity | Duration | Content | Tools |
|----------|----------|---------|-------|
| Arduino basics | 4 hours | Sensors and outputs | Arduino Uno |
| 3D printing intro | 4 hours | Design and print a creation | Tinkercad + 3D printer |
| Scratch game | 6 hours | Design a complete game | Scratch |

---

### 3.4 Exploration Stage (T1: Ages 13-15)

#### Developmental Characteristics

| Dimension | Characteristics | Design Implications |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Cognition** | Formal operational beginning | Abstract thinking possible |
| **Autonomy** | Pursuing independence | More self-direction |
| **Peers** | Strong peer influence | Community belonging |
| **Identity** | Self-exploration | Deepening interests |
| **Critical thinking** | Beginning to question | Encourage critical thought |

#### Design Principles

| Principle | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Self-directed choice** | Broader choice space | Self-selected project themes |
| **Deep challenges** | More complex projects | Integrated projects |
| **Real-world problems** | Connect to real world | Community problem solving |
| **Peer learning** | Community and collaboration | Project teams, mentorship |
| **Reflection & critique** | Metacognitive development | Reflection journals, design reviews |

#### Activity Examples

| Activity | Duration | Content | Tools |
|----------|----------|---------|-------|
| Python programming | 16 hours | Text-based programming intro | Python + IDE |
| Robotics project | 20 hours | Design and build a robot | Arduino + mechanical components |
| IoT project | 16 hours | Sensor networks | ESP32 + cloud platform |

### 3.5 Development Stage (T2: Ages 16-18)

#### Developmental Characteristics

| Dimension | Characteristics | Design Implications |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Cognition** | Mature formal operations | Complex abstract tasks |
| **Planning** | Capable of long-term planning | Long-cycle projects |
| **Specialization** | Clear domain interests | Professional deepening |
| **Autonomy** | Highly autonomous | Independent research |
| **Social** | Social responsibility | Social innovation projects |

#### Design Principles

| Principle | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Independent research** | Autonomous inquiry | Research projects |
| **Mentorship** | Expert guidance | One-on-one mentoring |
| **Professional depth** | Domain specialization | Specialized courses |
| **Social connection** | Real social problems | Social innovation projects |
| **Academic bridge** | University preparation | Research methodology training |

#### Activity Examples

| Activity | Duration | Content | Tools |
|----------|----------|---------|-------|
| AI/ML project | 40 hours | Machine learning applications | Python + TensorFlow |
| Comprehensive research | 60+ hours | Independent research project | Project-dependent |
| Startup project | Open-ended | Product prototype development | Comprehensive |

### 3.6 Adult Stage (A: 18+)

#### Design Characteristics

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Autonomy** | Fully self-directed, learning on demand |
| **Practicality** | Solving real problems |
| **Efficiency** | Time is precious, efficient learning |
| **Specialization** | Professional depth or cross-disciplinary learning |

#### Design Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Need-driven** | Start from practical needs |
| **Modular** | Select modules as needed |
| **Community support** | Like-minded community |
| **Learn and apply** | Immediate application |

## 4. Differentiated Teaching Strategies

### 4.1 Age-Adapting a Single Activity

Using "LED Circuits" as an example:

| Age | Content Adjustment | Scaffolding | Output |
|-----|-------------------|-------------|--------|
| **K** | Sticker circuits | Templates, parent help | Light up one LED |
| **J1** | Breadboard circuits | Step cards, demonstrations | Series/parallel circuits |
| **J2** | Arduino-controlled | Code templates | Programmable lights |
| **T1** | Self-designed | Principle explanations | Interactive light installation |

### 4.2 Mixed-Age Learning Design

| Strategy | Description | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Older guides younger** | Older students mentor younger ones | Peer tutoring system |
| **Layered tasks** | Same project, different tasks | Each person has their role |
| **Multiple roles** | Different abilities, different roles | Designer, maker, tester |
| **Open-ended outcomes** | Allow different levels of output | Works of varying complexity |

## 5. Assessment and Adaptation

### 5.1 Age-Appropriateness Checklist

| Checkpoint | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| **Cognitive match** | Is the level of abstraction appropriate? |
| **Skill match** | Is the operational difficulty appropriate? |
| **Duration fit** | Is the activity duration suitable? |
| **Interest relevance** | Is the topic engaging for this age group? |
| **Safety suitability** | Are materials and operations safe? |

### 5.2 Adjustment Strategies

| Issue | Adjustment Strategy |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Too difficult** | Add scaffolding, simplify steps, lower expectations |
| **Too easy** | Add challenges, reduce support, raise expectations |
| **Not enough time** | Segment, simplify, focus on core |
| **Low interest** | Change context, add gamification, connect to student interests |
