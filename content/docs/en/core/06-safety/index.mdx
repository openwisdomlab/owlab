## Building Standards

<Callout type="info">
The core of M06 is "safety empowers more risk-taking." Assessment criteria are designed around **whether three lines of defense are in place, whether a safety culture has formed, and whether ethical red lines are maintained**, making safety a guarantee for creativity rather than a constraint.
</Callout>

### Core Principles

| ID | Core Principle | One-Liner | Practice Signals |
|----|---------------|-----------|-----------------|
| **N1** | Managed Risk | Don't pursue zero risk; pursue "safe failure" | Learners dare to attempt challenging projects |
| **N2** | Three Lines of Defense | Equipment, process, personnel layered together | Single-point errors don't lead to accidents |
| **N3** | Tiered Controls | Different risks get different treatment | Green/Yellow/Red tiers clearly visible |
| **N4** | Ethical Red Lines | AI usage has boundaries | AI assists not replaces; generated content labeled |
| **N5** | Safety Culture | Safety is consensus, not command | Learners proactively comply rather than passively obey |

### Mandatory Standards (10 items)

| ID | Standard | Why It Matters | Quantitative Metric | Tier |
|----|----------|---------------|---------------------|------|
| **S1** | Fire Compliance | Physical safety baseline | Fire extinguishers, smoke detectors, evacuation routes 100% compliant | Foundation |
| **S2** | Equipment Tier Labels | Risk must be visible to be managed | All equipment has Green/Yellow/Red tier labels | Foundation |
| **S3** | Safety Training Coverage | Must know rules to follow them | New member safety training coverage 100% | Foundation |
| **S4** | First Aid Equipment Ready | Emergency response assurance | First aid kit equipped and within validity | Foundation |
| **S5** | Minor Protection | Special protection for special groups | Two-person rule and parental consent 100% enforced | Foundation |
| **S6** | Emergency Drills | Must practice to be prepared | >= 1 fire/emergency drill per year | Growth |
| **S7** | Incident/Near-Miss Reporting | Learn from events | 100% of incidents and near-misses recorded and reported | Growth |
| **S8** | AI Ethics Standards | Guard technological ethical boundaries | AI usage standards publicly posted; generated content labeling enforced | Growth |
| **S9** | Safety Culture Atmosphere | From passive to proactive | >= 70% of learners can state core safety rules | Excellence |
| **S10** | Continuous Improvement Records | Iterative safety system optimization | Annual safety review with documented improvement actions | Excellence |

### Parameter Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Healthy Range | Warning Signal |
|--------|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Safety Incident Rate** | Injury-causing incidents / Total activities x 1000 | 0-1 per mille | >2 per mille requires immediate investigation |
| **Near-Miss Reports** | Average monthly near-miss reports | 2-5 times | 0 times may mean "afraid to report" not "nothing happened" |
| **Safety Training Coverage** | People completing safety training / Total active users x 100% | 100% | &lt;90% blind spots exist |

### Extension Patterns

| Pattern | Applicable Context | Core Practice | Expected Outcome |
|---------|-------------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Safety Ambassador Program** | Strengthening safety culture | Train learners as "Safety Ambassadors" responsible for daily safety reminders and newcomer guidance | Safety goes from "management's job" to "everyone's job" |
| **Near-Miss Learning Sessions** | Learning from events | Regularly review near-miss incidents, analyze causes, propose improvements | Plug holes before accidents happen |

### Assessment Tools

**Monthly Safety Baseline Checklist**

| Checkpoint | Yes | No |
|------------|-----|----|
| Fire extinguishers within validity and pressure normal? | [] | [] |
| Evacuation routes clear, no obstructions? | [] | [] |
| All Yellow/Red equipment has tier labels? | [] | [] |
| First aid kit supplies complete and within validity? | [] | [] |
| All new members this month completed safety training? | [] | [] |

**Swiss Cheese Three Lines of Defense Self-Check**

```text
+-------------------------------------------------------------+
|  Three Lines of Defense Check                                |
+-------------------------------------------------------------+
|  First Line: Equipment Defense                               |
|  [] Hazardous equipment has physical guards/barriers         |
|  [] High-risk equipment has emergency stop buttons,          |
|     functioning properly                                     |
|  [] Electrical equipment has residual current protection     |
+-------------------------------------------------------------+
|  Second Line: Process Defense                                |
|  [] Written Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) exist      |
|  [] High-risk operations have checklists                     |
|  [] Yellow/Red equipment has training certification          |
+-------------------------------------------------------------+
|  Third Line: Personnel Defense                               |
|  [] All users have completed safety training                 |
|  [] Clear emergency contacts and procedures established      |
|  [] Near-miss reporting encouraged, no-blame policy          |
+-------------------------------------------------------------+
```

**AI Ethics Red Line Check**

| Red Line Rule | Implementation Status |
|--------------|----------------------|
| AI-generated content must cite its source | [] Posted [] Supervised [] Documented cases |
| Private information never uploaded to public AI | [] Trained [] Reminded [] Checked |
| AI assists not replaces; final judgment by humans | [] Discussed [] Consensus reached [] Documented cases |

**Quick Safety Culture Test**

Ask 5 random learners:
1. Where is the first aid kit?
2. What do you do in case of fire?
3. What color tier is the laser cutter?
4. What must you do when using AI-generated content?

```text
Correct answer reference:
>= 80%: Safety culture is strong
60-80%: Needs more reinforcement
&lt;60%: Safety culture not established, immediate improvement needed
```

> **One-line summary**: The prerequisite for daring to take risks — safety and boundaries. When learners know the environment is safe, they actually dare more to try new things.

| 3E Dimension | How This Module Embodies It |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Enlighten** | Understanding risk is itself scientific literacy — learning to assess, to respect, and to be brave within boundaries |
| **Empower** | Swiss Cheese Model + three safety tiers + complete SOPs, systematic protection making risk manageable and failure bearable |
| **Engage** | Build a safety culture maintained by the community — safety is not management's command but the creator's consensus |

Safety is not a restriction on creation but the prerequisite for creativity to be fully unleashed. This module constructs a "physical-digital-ethical" three-layer defense system, aiming to manage risk through **system design** rather than relying solely on individual vigilance. For OWL, a global network serving young people, safety compliance is not just an operational red line but a brand lifeline.
