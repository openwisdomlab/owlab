# Safety Training System

## Overview

Safety training is not a one-time event — it is an ongoing culture. This document establishes a comprehensive, tiered safety training system that ensures every person in the OWL innovation space has the knowledge and skills to protect themselves and others.

**Core Philosophy**: Safety is a prerequisite for risk-taking and bold exploration. When people feel safe, they are more willing to experiment.

---

## 1. Training System Architecture

### 1.1 Four-Level Training Structure

| Level | Name | Target | Duration | Prerequisite |
|-------|------|--------|----------|-------------|
| **T1** | Entry | All new users | 45-60 min | None |
| **T2** | Basic | Equipment users | 2-4 hours | T1 complete |
| **T3** | Advanced | High-risk equipment | 4-8 hours | T2 + supervised hours |
| **T4** | Expert | Trainers, managers | 16+ hours | T3 + experience |

### 1.2 Training Audience and Pathways

| Audience | Required Levels | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Casual visitor | Abbreviated T1 | Safety orientation only |
| Regular learner | T1 → T2 (relevant modules) | Progressive training |
| Advanced maker | T1 → T2 → T3 (specific equipment) | Certification-based |
| Educator/volunteer | T1 → T2 → T3 → T4 (TTT) | Train-the-trainer |
| Lab manager | All levels | Comprehensive certification |

## 2. T1 Entry-Level Training

### 2.1 Training Objectives

All participants can identify hazards, use basic safety equipment, and respond to emergencies.

### 2.2 Course Outline

| Module | Content | Duration | Method |
|--------|---------|----------|--------|
| Welcome & rules | Lab rules, code of conduct, expectations | 10 min | Presentation |
| Hazard awareness | Types of hazards in the space | 10 min | Interactive tour |
| Emergency procedures | Fire exits, first aid, emergency stops | 10 min | Walk-through |
| PPE basics | When and how to use safety gear | 10 min | Hands-on |
| Reporting | How to report incidents and near-misses | 5 min | Discussion |
| Assessment | Knowledge check quiz | 10 min | Written/digital |

### 2.3 Children's T1 Training (Ages 7-12)

| Adaptation | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Shorter duration** | 30 minutes maximum |
| **Visual-heavy** | Pictures, videos, demonstrations |
| **Interactive** | Safety treasure hunt, role-play scenarios |
| **Simple language** | Age-appropriate vocabulary |
| **Parent involvement** | Parents attend alongside children |
| **Repeated reinforcement** | Brief safety reminders each visit |

### 2.4 Assessment and Certification

- Quiz: 80% pass threshold (children: oral assessment)
- Certificate issued: valid for 1 year
- Record in user profile
- Annual renewal required

## 3. T2 Basic-Level Training

### 3.1 Training Objectives

Participants can safely operate S2 equipment and use appropriate PPE for each activity.

### 3.2 Modular Courses

#### T2-A Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

| PPE Type | When Required | Proper Use |
|----------|--------------|-----------|
| Safety glasses | All shop activities | Fit check, cleaning |
| Hearing protection | Power tools, CNC | Proper insertion/fit |
| Gloves | Material handling, chemicals | Type selection by hazard |
| Dust mask | Sanding, dusty materials | Seal check, filter selection |

#### T2-B Electrical Safety Basics

| Topic | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Electrical hazards | Shock, burns, fire risks |
| Safe practices | Disconnect before working, dry hands, proper cords |
| Voltage awareness | Safe vs. dangerous voltage levels |
| Emergency response | Power cut-off, first aid for shock |

#### T2-C 3D Printing Safety

| Topic | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Thermal hazards | Hot nozzle (200°C+), heated bed (60-110°C) |
| Material safety | PLA (safe), ABS (ventilation needed), resin (PPE required) |
| Fire safety | Don't leave prints unattended; smoke detector nearby |
| Mechanical hazards | Moving parts, pinch points |

#### T2-D Electronics Soldering Basics

| Topic | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Burn prevention | Iron handling, cooling procedures |
| Fume extraction | Always use fume extractor |
| Lead safety | Lead-free solder preferred; hand washing |
| Tool care | Tip tinning, proper resting |

### 3.3 Assessment Standards

| Criterion | Method | Pass Threshold |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Knowledge | Written quiz | 80% |
| Practical skills | Demonstration | Satisfactory performance |
| Safety judgment | Scenario questions | Correct response |

## 4. T3 Advanced-Level Training

### 4.1 Training Objectives

Participants can independently operate S3 equipment and respond to equipment-specific emergencies.

### 4.2 High-Risk Equipment Training

#### T3-LASER: Laser Cutter Training

| Module | Content | Duration |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Theory | Laser types, beam hazards, material interactions | 1 hour |
| Materials | Approved materials, banned materials (PVC!), material prep | 30 min |
| Operation | Start-up, parameter setting, job execution, shutdown | 1 hour |
| Safety systems | Interlocks, exhaust, fire suppression | 30 min |
| Emergency | Fire response, beam exposure, fume exposure | 30 min |
| Practical exam | Complete a cutting job independently | 1 hour |

#### T3-CNC: CNC Operation Training (18+ only)

| Module | Content | Duration |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Theory | CNC principles, coordinate systems, tooling | 1 hour |
| Safety | Mechanical hazards, dust, noise, emergency stops | 30 min |
| CAM basics | Toolpath generation, feeds and speeds | 1 hour |
| Operation | Workholding, tool changes, job execution | 1.5 hours |
| Practical exam | Complete a milling job with supervision | 1 hour |

### 4.3 First Aid Training

#### T3-FA: First Responder Certification

| Module | Content | Duration |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Scene assessment | Assessing safety, calling for help | 30 min |
| Wound care | Cuts, burns, foreign bodies | 1 hour |
| CPR basics | Chest compressions, AED use | 1 hour |
| Special injuries | Chemical exposure, electric shock, eye injuries | 1 hour |
| Practice scenarios | Role-play emergency responses | 1 hour |

---

## 5. T4 Expert-Level Training

### 5.1 Training Objectives

Participants can train others, manage safety systems, and lead emergency responses.

### 5.2 Train-the-Trainer (TTT)

| Module | Content | Duration |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Adult learning theory | How adults learn differently | 2 hours |
| Training delivery | Presentation skills, demonstration techniques | 4 hours |
| Assessment design | Creating effective assessments | 2 hours |
| Handling difficult situations | Resistant learners, safety violations | 2 hours |
| Practice teaching | Deliver a training module with feedback | 4 hours |

### 5.3 Safety Management Training

| Topic | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Risk assessment | Conducting and documenting risk assessments |
| Incident investigation | Root cause analysis, corrective actions |
| Compliance | Regulatory requirements, documentation |
| Safety culture | Building and maintaining a safety-first culture |

### 5.4 Emergency Command Training

| Topic | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Incident command | Roles, communication, decision-making |
| Evacuation management | Routes, accountability, special needs |
| Communication | Internal notification, external agencies |
| Post-incident | Documentation, debriefing, improvement |

## 6. Training Implementation Management

### 6.1 Training Formats

| Format | Best For | Advantages | Limitations |
|--------|---------|-----------|------------|
| **In-person** | Practical skills, equipment training | Hands-on, immediate feedback | Scheduling, space |
| **Online modules** | Theory, knowledge refreshers | Flexible, scalable | No hands-on |
| **Blended** | Comprehensive programs | Best of both | Requires both resources |
| **Just-in-time** | Point-of-use reminders | Contextual, brief | Limited depth |
| **Peer training** | Community building | Social learning | Quality control |

### 6.2 Training Resources

| Resource | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Safety manual | Comprehensive reference document |
| Quick-reference cards | Laminated cards at each workstation |
| Video library | Demonstration videos for each equipment type |
| Quiz bank | Assessment questions by topic |
| Incident case studies | Real examples for discussion |

### 6.3 Training Records

```text
Training Record Components:
├── Trainee information (name, age, contact)
├── Training completed (level, module, date)
├── Assessment results (score, pass/fail)
├── Certifications held (type, issued, expiry)
├── Incidents involved (if any)
└── Renewal due dates
```

### 6.4 Training Effectiveness Evaluation

| Level | What to Evaluate | Method |
|-------|-----------------|--------|
| **Reaction** | Did trainees find it useful? | Feedback survey |
| **Learning** | Did trainees learn the content? | Pre/post quiz |
| **Behavior** | Are trainees applying the training? | Observation |
| **Results** | Has safety improved? | Incident rate tracking |

## 7. Special Scenario Training

### 7.1 New Staff Onboarding

- Complete all T1-T3 relevant modules within first 2 weeks
- Shadow experienced staff for 1 week
- Supervised solo operation for 2 weeks
- Full certification by end of month 1

### 7.2 Seasonal/Holiday Training

| Period | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| Summer camps | Abbreviated training for short-term participants |
| Holiday events | Safety briefing for visitors and guests |
| New school year | Refresher training for returning users |

### 7.3 Post-Incident Retraining

- Mandatory for anyone involved in a safety incident
- Tailored to the specific incident type
- Includes root cause discussion
- Re-assessment before reinstatement

## 9. Safety Culture Building

### 9.1 Safety Culture Core Values

| Value | Meaning | Practice |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Safety is everyone's responsibility** | Not just the manager's job | Peer observation, reporting |
| **Near-misses matter** | Learning from what almost happened | No-blame reporting |
| **Continuous improvement** | Safety systems evolve | Regular reviews, updates |
| **Safe to speak up** | Reporting is encouraged, not punished | Psychological safety |

### 9.2 Diversity and Inclusivity

Safety training must be accessible to all:
- Multi-language materials where needed
- Visual instructions for diverse literacy levels
- Accommodations for physical disabilities
- Cultural sensitivity in examples and scenarios
- Gender-inclusive language and examples

### 9.3 "Safe to Fail" Culture

The safety system exists to enable bold exploration:
- Clear boundaries make experimentation safer
- Known risks can be managed; unknown risks are dangerous
- Safety training builds confidence to take creative risks
- "What's the worst that can happen?" should have a good answer

## 10. 2024-2025 Training Trends

- AI-generated training scenarios customized to the learner
- Chatbot-based safety Q&A available 24/7
